I am a PhD cadidate in Computer Science at [Stony Brook University](https://www.stonybrook.edu/) advised by [Supartha Podder](https://www.suparthapodder.com/). My research mainly focuses on the structure needed for quantum speedups and some of my current research interests include representation theory, quantum cryptography, and algebraic complexity. Previously, I attended the [University of Minnesota](https://twin-cities.umn.edu/) where I recieved my BS in mathematics and participated in the [AMAAZE](https://www.amaaze.org/) group, under the supervision of [Jeff Calder](https://www-users.cse.umn.edu/~jwcalder/index.html), developing mathematical and software tools for the analysis of bone fragements, as well as the [Next Generation Robotics Lab](https://nextgenai.umn.edu/) under [Maria Gini](https://www-users.cse.umn.edu/~gini/) developing natural language processing tools for medical and home care facing use cases. I highly reccomend you go check out both!
